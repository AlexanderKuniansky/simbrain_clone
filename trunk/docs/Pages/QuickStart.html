Quick Start

Run a simulation. When Simbrain launches, simply press play in the network toolbar and observe activity propagating through the network and the creature moving. Open different simulations and press play in each case to see what happens. Some good simulations to play with are behavior.sim and cage.sim. As a simulation runs, try selecting and deleting neurons to see what impact this has on the creature's behavior.

Manually moving the creature around. Try clicking play, , and moving the creature around different objects. This is like picking up the creature and placing it in different places. Try this (1) with the interaction button set to "both ways" and, (2) with it set to "world to net" only . In the first case you are testing sensormotor coordination; in the second case you are only testing the way the network reacts to stimuli. Try the simulation wta_generalization.sim, and note that the creature responds similarly to different flowers even though they stimulate it slightly differently.

Studying a network on its own. (Set network-world interaction to ) Open a network, for example one of the ring networks, and press the step button (or the space bar) to see how it works. Open other networks, lasso nodes, and manually adjust activation with the up an down arrow buttons. Then press the step button or space bar to see what happens to the activation.

Finding stable states. (Set network-world interaction to ) While in the network component, select all nodes with "N", press "R" to randomize the nodes and press the space bar a few times to update the network until it settles into a stable state (that is, until the nodes don't change when you press space). Repeat; press "R" and "space, space,space" repeatedly until the net settles in a stable state, then "R" and "space, space, space." again, etc. In this way get a sense of the stable states of that network.

Build or edit a network. Clear the screen by selecting "New network" from the Network menu and create a few neurons using the button. Then connect them as described here.

Study network parameters. Select all nodes with "N", then double-click on a node. Change it's decay value, activation function, etc. Then move the creature around a bit (by left dragging) to see how network dynamics have changed.

Study learning. Select all weights with "W", then double-click on a weight. Change it's learning rule or other parameters. Then move the creature around a bit to see how learning (change of weights over time) is affected.

Use Gauges. Open a gauge by pressing the add gauge button and open the simulation simple.sim or loops.sim. Drag the creature around the different objects and watch the patterns that emerge in the gauge. Try opening different worlds and gauges and seeing what patterns show up in the gauge. When a pattern has developed for a period of time, switch the gauge projection chooser from "coordinate" to "PCA" and from "PCA" to "Sammon." When it is in Sammon press the play button inside that gauge to see what patterns emerge.
