Integrate and and Fire

The integrate and fire (this is technically the "leaky integrate and fire") model is perhaps the most prevalent biologically realistic model neuron in use currently. It simulates all the basic aspects of neural conduction in a relatively simple framework. It is a spiking neuron, and as such its border will turn yellow when a spike event occurs.

Activation of the neuron is interpreted as voltage potential. When that voltage potential passes a threshold value, the neuron spikes, and then the voltage is reset to a reset value, typically below the threshold potential. Below threshold, the neuron behaves like the additive neuron. It decays to a steady state value at a rate proporitional to its time constant.

Resting potential

The activation value which the neuron decays to over time.

Reset potential

The activation value which the neuron is reset to after a spiking event.

Threshold

The value beyond which the neuron spikes.

Time constant

Controls the rate at which the neuron attains its resting potential. Make it larger and things slow down; make it lower and things speed up.

Resistance

A constant factor multiplied by weighted inputs.
