Simbrain design goals:

  • User can immediately begin experimenting with neural networks.
  • Easy-to-use, visual GUI which draws on familiar interface elements.
  • Provide an educational tool for learning about neural networks through direct experimentation and visual representation.
  • Ability to study sensorimotor integration by embedding the network in a virtual world.
  • Ability to rapidly prototype neural networks of any topology.
  • Ability to view the topological structure of the state-space of a neural network in real-time, and thereby study its representational capacities.

What Simbrain is not:

  • An engineering tool or a package for physiologically realistic modeling. In these cases, other programs are better suited; see below.
  • Currently not a suitable platform for large simulations involving hundreds or thousands of neurons.
Comparison with other neural network packages:

Genesis, Neuron
Genesis and Neuron are geared towards modeling biophysically realistic neurons. The emphasis is on analysis of single neurons or small groups of neurons. Simbrain is more focused on analysis of networks of simplified connectionist processing units.

JNNS, Joone, Open-AI
JNNS, Joone, and Open-AI are powerful applications which operate at the same network level as Simbrain. JNNS (a java-interface to SNNS) is the oldest and most powerful freeware java neural network, contains numerous learning algorithms and supported by a large user-base. SNNS is not, however, open-source. Joone is open-source, and its focus is on backpropagation.

PDP++
Heir to the PDP simulation package that came with "volume 3" of the original PDP volumes. Not open source.

Catacomb
Catacomb is a comprehensive, open-source, java-based program for modeling realistic neural networks and virtual environments. It is similar to Simbrain in scope and intent, though its emphasis is less on connectionism and more on integrate-and-fire units. Also, the "feel" of the interface is different.
