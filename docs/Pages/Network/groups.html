Groups

These are  logical groupings of neurons and / or synapses.   There are many reasons to collection neurons and synpases together. For example, it can be useful to move, randomize, or other permute or edit of a sub-population of synapses, that would otherwise be hard to access and modify as a units

Perhaps the most important groups are subnetworks, which are discussed separately.

Subnetworks are distinguished by being surrounded by a gray line, with a special tab, often in the upper left-hand corner. is the your interface with the subnetwork and its properties.  If you right click on the tab, various actions appear.   Here is an example of a group, a backprop network.  Note that it is a group of groups: it includes as sub-groups 3 neuron groups, and 2 synapse groups.  Each group has a yellow interaction tab.

All groups have the following actions defined:

Remove group

Delete the group and all its constituents.

Edit group name

Edit the name of the group which appears in the interaction tab.

Double Click

If you double-click on the subnetwork tab it calls up that network's properties dialog. These properties depend on the subnetwork type.
