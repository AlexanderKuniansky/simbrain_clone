DataWorld

The simplest world type, a "dataworld" which is simply a spreadsheet which sends data to worlds in the form of rows on a table. Inputting data into DataWorld is as simple as entering data into a spreadsheet. Rows represent patterns or vectors of data, and columns represent components of that vector. A send button at the beginning of each row initiates a sensory or motor transaction. Sensory neurons are connected to particular columns, and will then register the value from the currently selected row when the send button is pressed. Motor neurons are also connected to particular columns, and output their value to that column of the currenty selected row.

To attach a column to a neuron, right click on the neuron to be attached, go down the popup menu to sensors, then follow the sub-menus to the DataWorld and finally to the column number to attach the neuron to. See couplings.
