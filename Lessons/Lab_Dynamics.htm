Other Experiments and Notes

By making larger neural networks, with more nodes, you are likely to get more fixed points and more interesting limit cycles.

Conceptually, you can think of each setting of the weights as a point in the 16 dimensional weight space (ok, I just gave away the answer to number 2). Each point in weight space is in turn associated with different numbers of limit cycles and attractors, in activation space. One interesting topic in dynamical systems theory is "bifurcation theory." In this context, this amounts to slowly changing the weights until you get a change in the number of attractors / limit cycles that can be identified in a network.
