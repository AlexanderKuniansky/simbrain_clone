Overview

What follows is an introduction to neural networks in cognitive science.

What are neural networks you ask? Very briefly, they are simulated brain or brain-like circuits. They are used in a variety of ways in a wide range of fields, including engineering, biology, psychology, and cognitive science. Our focus is on neural networks in cognitive science.

Often, introductory courses or texts in neural networks are math intensive, requiring calculus and linear algebra, which is appropriate, since they are mathematical models. But neural networks aren't really that complicated, and given their importance to understanding the mind and brain, there is no reason a wider range of people shouldn't be able to study them. This is an effort to teach neural networks with minimal mathematics; in particular, no calculus. Technical details are included as appendices where appropriate. In order to make things even easier to deal with, this course is designed to be used in conjunction with a computer program, Simbrain, though of course the text can be read independently from that. As you go along you can try to test some of these ideas by using Simbrain simulations or creating simualations of your own in Simbrain.

So, you might think of what follows as "neural networks without calculus," or perhaps even "neural networks for poets."
